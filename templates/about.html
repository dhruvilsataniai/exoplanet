About the Exoplanet Detection AI System

Project Overview

The Exoplanet Detection AI System is an advanced machine learning platform designed to identify and classify exoplanets using data from NASA's premier space missions. By leveraging cutting-edge algorithms and comprehensive datasets from the Kepler, K2, and TESS missions, our system achieves remarkable accuracy in distinguishing between confirmed exoplanets, planetary candidates, and false positives.

This project represents the intersection of astronomy, data science, and artificial intelligence, providing researchers and enthusiasts with powerful tools to explore the cosmos and contribute to the ongoing search for worlds beyond our solar system.

Mission

Democratize exoplanet discovery through accessible AI-powered analysis tools

Technical Architecture

Data Pipeline

Automated data fetching from NASA Exoplanet Archive with comprehensive preprocessing, feature engineering, and quality validation.

  • Data visualization

Data Sources

Kepler Mission

NASA's first planet-hunting mission, which observed over 150,000 stars and discovered thousands of exoplanet candidates through the transit method.

  • Duration: 2009-2017
  • Targets: 150,000+ stars
  • Discoveries: 2,600+ confirmed planets
K2 Mission

Extended mission of Kepler, observing different fields in the ecliptic plane, expanding the search for exoplanets to various stellar populations.

  • Duration: 2014-2018
  • Campaigns: 19 observing campaigns
  • Discoveries: 500+ confirmed planets
TESS Mission

Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite, conducting an all-sky survey to find transiting exoplanets around the brightest stars near Earth.

  • Launch: 2018-present
  • Coverage: All-sky survey
  • Focus: Nearby bright stars
